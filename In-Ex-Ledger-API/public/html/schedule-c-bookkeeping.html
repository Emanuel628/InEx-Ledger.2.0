U.S. solo operators

Schedule C bookkeeping that feels less like cleanup season and more like control.

If your business files on Schedule C, the hard part is rarely the form itself. The hard part is keeping the ledger clean enough that the numbers, receipts, mileage, and exclusions still make sense when someone reviews them later.

InEx Ledger is built for that middle ground. You can track the money, attach support, catch what is missing, and export a package that is more CPA hand-off ready.

What matters

Cleaner records before the handoff.

Schedule C bookkeeping breaks down when expense support is scattered, owner draws are mixed into business activity, and imported transactions never get a proper review. This workflow is designed to surface those problems before tax time.

Where it helps

What solo businesses usually need from a Schedule C bookkeeping system

Expense support that stays attached

Receipts, support files, and notes stay with the transaction instead of living in a separate folder that only makes sense to you.

Vehicle records that do not get guessed later

Mileage and support requirements can stay visible while you work, instead of turning into a scramble after the year closes.

Review-ready exports

When you need to hand records over, you can generate PDF and CSV workpapers with blockers, warnings, totals, and excluded items spelled out.

What to keep clean

The usual Schedule C trouble spots are not mysterious

  • Imported transactions that still need a real category
  • Expenses without receipts or missing business-purpose notes
  • Vehicle rows without mileage support
  • Transfers, owner draws, and tax payments that should not distort the profit and loss view
  • Exports that do not explain what is missing
How InEx Ledger fits
